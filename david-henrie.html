David Henrie Biography

David Henrie was born on July 11, 1989, in Mission Viejo, California, United States. David Henrie is the son of Linda Henrie, his mother, and James Wilson Henrie, his father. His mother manages talent, and his father is a producer.

He has one sibling, a younger brother named Lorenzo James Henrie, who is also an actor. David Henrie is of mixed descent, with English, Welsh, German, and Swiss-German ancestors on his father’s side and Italian ancestors on his mother’s.

He was raised in Phoenix, Arizona, and attended the Roman Catholic Church. He has stated that he is and will always be a Christian.

David Henrie Wife and marrige

David Henrie is a happily married man with a straight sexual orientation. Henrie’s wife’s name is Maria Cahill, and they married on April 21, 2017, in a wedding that brought the Wizards of Waverly Place costars back together after a long time apart.

They have two children with his wife: James Thomas Augustin Emanuel, their son, and Pia Philomena Francesca, their daughter. Henrie announced in December 2021 that he and his wife were expecting their third child.

Career

In terms of his career, David Henrie began his career when he was 13 years old, after landing a regular role in the Fox sitcom The Pitts. In this sitcom, he portrayed Petey Pitt. Following that, he appeared in films such as Hallmark Movie, Dead Hollywood Moms Society, and Monster Markers.

He has also appeared on Without a Trace, Providence, Judging Amy, The Mullets, Jack & Bobby, The D.A., House, NCIS, and Cold Case. He had recurring roles in both That’s So Raven and How I Met Your Mother, playing Cory’s friend Larry in That’s So Raven and Ted’s future son in How I Met Your Mother.

He played the role of Justin Russo in the Disney Channel Original Series Wizards of Waverly Place when he was 18 years old. This show contributed significantly to his popularity. He also wrote two episodes of this show, Meet the Werewolves and Alex’s Logo.

Later on, he appeared in shows like Disney Channel Original Movie Dadnapped, Jonas, Easy to Assemble in 2010, The Secret World of Arrietty in 2012, Grown Ups 2 in 2013, 1000 to 1: The Cory Weissman Story in 2014, Mind Games in 2014, and directed two short films called Catch and Boo! in 2014.

He later appeared in Paul Blart: Mall Cop 2, Little Boy, Walt Before Mickey, Cardboard Boxer, and Warrior Road. In 2018, he also directed the film This Is the Year. In the year 2020, he and his Wizards of Waverly Place co-star Selena Gomez announced the release of a virtual movie called This is the Year.
